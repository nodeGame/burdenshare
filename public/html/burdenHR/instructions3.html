In Stage Two, you will play four rounds. Each round will be played against one other participant either in the role of a "proposer" or in the role of a "responder". These roles are randomly assigned to each player every round.

You will start each round with the accumulated wealth and climate risk from the first stage. The wealth amongst players ranges between 30 (e.g. low growth) and 125 ECU (e.g. high growth). Players contribution to overall climate risk can be between 0 and 25 percent. Remember that those who had higher economic growth in the past will have larger contributions to climate risk.

In each round, the proposer will be asked how much of the total cost she/he offers to pay. This offer has to be an integer number between 0 and . After that, the responder will be asked whether she/he accepts or rejects the offer.

If the responder accepts, a climate agreement has been reached and each player's share of the cost is subtracted from his/her wealth.

If the responder rejects, there is no agreement and no costs have to be paid. However, there is a risk that a climate catastrophe will occur. This risk is the sum of both players' contribution to climate risk plus a baseline risk of 15 percent that means climate risk is between 15% and 65%.

If a catastrophe happens each player loses half of his or her wealth. The computer randomly determines whether a catastrophe occurs in case of no agreement.
