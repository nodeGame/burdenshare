You will play a total of four rounds. Each round follows the same format. In each round of the experiment, you will play against a different person participating in the experiment. One of you will be randomly assigned the role of “proposer” and the other will randomly be assigned the role of "responder". At the beginning of the round, you will learn the wealth of your one-person economy. The wealth amongst players ranges between 30 and 125 ECU reflecting different rates of economic growth in the past.

At the start of each round, the proposer will be asked how much of the total cost they are willing to pay. This offer has to be a whole number between 0 and . After that, the responder will be asked whether they accept or reject the offer. If the responder accepts and a climate agreement has been reached, then each player's share of the cost is subtracted from their overall wealth.

If the responder rejects, there is no agreement and no payments are made. However, there is a risk that a climate catastrophe will occur. Both players contribute to a potential climate catastrophe. The past economic growth in your one-person economy determines your contribution to climate risk. Players can increase the probability of a climate catastrophe between 0 to 25 percent. The overall risk is the sum of both players contribution plus a baseline risk of 15 percent. At the start of each round, you will learn your contribution to climate risk, the other player's contribution, and the added overall climate risk. If a catastrophe happens, each player looses half of their wealth. The game will randomly determine whether a catastrophe occurs in the cases of non-agreement.
